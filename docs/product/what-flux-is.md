# What Flux Behaviour is

Flux Behaviour is a consent-led behavioural analytics product for digital services.

It helps service teams understand where people struggle in real journeys by observing interaction metadata such as pauses, backtracking, repeated errors, help use, navigation patterns and recovery behaviour.

It does not record what people type.

It does not collect passwords, typed values, free-text content, names, direct identifiers or raw clipboard content.

It does not make eligibility, enforcement, fraud, benefit, immigration, policing or casework decisions.

## Product purpose

Flux Behaviour exists to help teams improve services that may pass formal checks but still cause users difficulty.

The source prototype described the product as a way to observe interaction signals such as dwell time, navigation patterns and form events only after explicit consent, mapping those signals to behavioural dimensions without personal data.

The product version keeps that intent but treats all prototype material as lineage, not production authority.

## What Flux Behaviour does

Flux Behaviour may:

- record consented interaction metadata;
- detect service friction, recovery behaviour and help-seeking patterns;
- support product teams, user researchers, service owners and governance reviewers;
- produce explainable service-improvement evidence;
- support human review of potential risks or barriers;
- contribute evidence for service assessment, accessibility follow-up and performance improvement.

## What Flux Behaviour does not do

Flux Behaviour must not:

- read or store field values;
- infer protected characteristics;
- profile users for enforcement or eligibility;
- replace user research;
- replace accessibility audits;
- replace security, privacy or DPIA review;
- surface personas to users in live services;
- present behavioural signals as a judgement about a person.

## Product interpretation rule

Behavioural signals must be framed as evidence about the service.

A pause may indicate confusion, reflection, cognitive load, careful checking, interruption or assistive technology interaction. The product must not reduce that behaviour to a single deficit explanation.

## Current repository status

This repository currently contains governance, contracts and validation scaffolding.

No runtime collector, dashboard, SDK or scoring engine is production-ready until promoted through a reviewed pull request.
